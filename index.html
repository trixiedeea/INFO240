Critocracy: A Critical Strategy Board Game

Game Overview

Critocracy is a strategy-based board game focused on decision-making, consequences, and role-based systems. Players take on ideological roles that influence how they interact with the game. The goal is to collect Money, Knowledge, and Influence. Each decision produces different outcomes depending on the player. The same situation can affect players in unequal ways. Players must think critically about each move. Survival depends on maintaining all three resources. The highest total wins at the end.

Game Board Design

The board consists of four selectable paths rather than a fixed route. Each path represents a different system of interaction and consequence. Players choose how they move instead of following a linear progression. Each space on the board triggers different outcomes. Some spaces provide advantages while others create risks. The structure ensures varied gameplay experiences. No two players experience the game the same way. Choices shape long-term outcomes.

The Age of Expansion

The Age of Expansion focuses on growth and resource accumulation. Players gain advantages through expansion-based decisions. However, these gains often come with increased risk. Some events reward aggressive strategies. Other events create resistance or setbacks. Different roles experience this path differently. The same event may benefit one player and harm another. This creates unequal consequences across the game.

The Age of Resistance

The Age of Resistance focuses on disruption and opposition to systems. Players encounter challenges that test adaptability. Knowledge and Influence are especially important here. Some outcomes reward resistance strategies. Others create instability or loss. The same situation affects players differently. Role abilities strongly influence results. Flexibility is key to success.

The Age of Reckoning

The Age of Reckoning focuses on consequences and accountability. Past actions influence current outcomes. Players must carefully manage all resources. Mistakes become more impactful in this path. Some events allow recovery from losses. Others increase difficulty and pressure. Each role experiences consequences differently. This path emphasizes responsibility and long-term thinking.

The Age of Legacy

The Age of Legacy focuses on interpretation and influence over time. Players interact with systems of memory and perception. Technology and communication affect outcomes. Some events generate resources through visibility. Others reduce resources through reinterpretation. Roles change how events are experienced. The same situation can produce different results. Meaning and control are central themes here.

Game Components

The game includes a modular board with four paths. Players use role cards to define abilities and starting resources. Resource tokens track Money, Knowledge, and Influence. Event decks introduce unpredictable situations. A dice controls movement across the board. Each component supports decision-based gameplay. The system encourages adaptability. Outcomes vary based on player choices.

Player Roles

Players choose from six roles with different strengths and abilities. Each role begins with unique starting resources. Roles interact differently with the same events. Some prioritize Money while others focus on Knowledge or Influence. Opposing roles create strategic tension. No role guarantees success. Role choice shapes overall strategy. Balance is essential for survival.

Setup

Players select roles at the start of the game. Each player receives starting resources based on their role. Event decks are shuffled and placed on the board. Paths are prepared before gameplay begins. Turn order is decided by a dice roll. Players are placed at the starting point. The game begins once setup is complete. All systems are ready for play.

Gameplay

Each turn begins with a dice roll. Players move along their chosen path. Regular spaces have no effect. Event spaces trigger card draws. All effects are resolved immediately. Some effects change resources directly. Others affect future decisions. The game continues until all players finish.

Strategic Mechanics

The game includes systems that emphasize consequences. Some cards allow resource interaction between players. Directly opposing roles cannot target each other. Each role has a unique ability. Some abilities increase gains while others reduce losses. Players can switch paths at designated points. Choices influence long-term outcomes. Strategy requires constant adaptation.

Victory Conditions

The game ends when all players reach the final space. Players may finish at different times. Resource totals are calculated at the end. Money, Knowledge, and Influence are combined. Players with zero resources are eliminated. The highest total wins. Success depends on survival and balance. Outcomes reflect long-term decisions.
