Critocracy: A Critical Strategy Board Game

Game Overview

Critocracy is a strategy-based board game focused on critical thinking, consequences, and decision-making under changing conditions. Players take on different ideological roles that shape how they interact with the game. The goal is to collect Money, Knowledge, and Influence. However, success depends on survival as well as accumulation. Players are eliminated if any resource reaches zero. Each situation affects players differently depending on their role. The game emphasizes perspective and consequence. The winner is the player with the highest total resources.

Game Board Design

The board consists of four selectable paths rather than a linear progression. Each path represents a different decision-making environment. Players choose how they move through the system instead of following a fixed order. Each path contains events that influence outcomes differently. The structure ensures that choices matter at every stage. Some paths reward risk while others reward stability. Players experience unequal consequences from identical situations. This reinforces the game’s focus on critical thinking.

The Age of Expansion

The Age of Expansion is a path focused on growth and accumulation. Players who choose this path tend to gain resources more quickly. However, expansion also increases exposure to risk. Events on this path reward aggressive decision-making. Some consequences appear later in the game. Different roles experience expansion differently. The same event may benefit one player and harm another. This creates uneven outcomes based on perspective.

The Age of Resistance

The Age of Resistance focuses on challenge and opposition to systems. Players encounter events involving conflict and disruption. Knowledge and Influence become especially important in this path. Some outcomes reward strategic resistance. Other outcomes create instability and loss. The same situation can affect players differently. Roles strongly influence how resistance is experienced. Adaptability is key to success in this path.

The Age of Reckoning

The Age of Reckoning focuses on consequences and accountability. Past decisions strongly influence current outcomes. Players must manage all three resources carefully. Mistakes become more significant in this path. Some events allow recovery while others increase loss. Each role interprets consequences differently. Identical events may produce unequal results. The path emphasizes long-term thinking and responsibility.

The Age of Legacy

The Age of Legacy focuses on interpretation and long-term impact. Players interact with systems that reflect memory and influence. Technology and communication shape outcomes in this path. Some events generate resources through visibility. Other events reduce resources through reinterpretation. Roles affect how Legacy is experienced. The same event may produce different results for each player. This path highlights how control over interpretation is powerful.

Game Components

The game includes a modular board with four selectable paths. Players use role cards to define abilities and starting stats. Resource tokens track Money, Knowledge, and Influence. Event decks introduce unpredictable challenges. A dice system controls movement. Each component supports variability and consequence-driven gameplay. The design encourages adaptation and strategic thinking. Every game unfolds differently depending on choices.

Player Roles

Players select from six roles that define gameplay style and strengths. Each role begins with different resource distributions. Roles interact differently with the same events. Some roles prioritize Money while others prioritize Knowledge or Influence. Opposing roles create strategic imbalance. These differences ensure varied outcomes. Role choice is central to strategy. No role guarantees success in all situations.

Setup

Players choose a role at the start of the game. Each player receives starting resources based on that role. Event decks are shuffled and placed on the board. The four paths are prepared before gameplay begins. Turn order is decided by a dice roll. Players are placed at the starting position. The game begins once setup is complete. All systems are ready for interaction and decision-making.

Gameplay

Each turn begins with a dice roll determining movement. Players move along their chosen path. Regular spaces have no effect. Event spaces trigger card draws. All effects must be resolved immediately. Some effects change resources directly. Others influence future decisions. The game continues until all players finish.

Strategic Mechanics

The game includes mechanics that emphasize consequence and interaction. Some cards allow resource stealing under specific conditions. Directly opposing roles cannot target each other. Each role has a unique ability. Some abilities increase gains while others reduce losses. Players can switch paths at designated points. Choices affect long-term outcomes. Strategy requires constant adaptation.

Victory Conditions

The game ends when all players reach the final space. Players may finish at different times. Resource totals are calculated at the end. Money, Knowledge, and Influence are combined. Players with zero resources are eliminated. The highest total score wins. Success requires balance and survival. Outcomes reflect long-term decisions.
